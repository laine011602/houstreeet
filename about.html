KOREAN STREET FOOD

The name "street food" evokes a sense of awe and a desire to eat it. South Korea's street food is well-known. Their street meal is known as "Pojangmacha" at night, and it's frequently served by "ajumma" (married women who are usually middle-aged or older) or a "ahjussi" (married man and usually middle-aged or older). The taste of street cuisine is just fantastic and unrivaled. It's a type of prepared food or drink offered on the street, in a market, fair, park or other public place that's tasty and ready to eat.

If South Korea is one of your travel destinations. You should not miss trying these incredible street foods that make your travel goals complete. This one's for the foodies out there: if food is your primary motivation for traveling, you'll be pleased to learn that South Korea is the ultimate foodie's paradise! What's more, you don't have to spend a fortune to fill your bellies. The place has all of your culinary demands addressed, from traditional meal establishments to hip cafe places, not to mention true street food. The majority of these foods are not only delicious, but they are also #bestfoods objectives!
